2. The Right Price

Web App and iPhone App / March of 2019

I developed a web application and iPhone app called “The Right Price” that helps local stores gain popularity by assisting customers in purchasing products of better quality at nearby locations. I was inspired to build this app after observing how online shopping services mostly target large retailers. This causes local businesses in small towns to lose out on customers. “The Right Price” app helps local businesses to gain popularity by assisting users in finding cheap, high-quality products nearby through the recommendations of their peers. The app achieves this goal by using Augmented Reality (AR) features on a smartphone and Crowdsourcing to guide the user to nearby locations where certain products are sold. The application creates virtual AR labels of those products in the viewing direction relative to the user’s current location by using the information obtained from other users about similar products. Therefore, the app serves as a virtual compass for the user and makes the shopping experience more entertaining and adventurous. This motivates users to purchase the products from local stores instead of buying the products online from large retailers. I presented my innovation at the Cupertino Hacks Hackathon and won 1st place. This gave me the opportunity to present the app at the Cupertino City Council on live TV to the Mayor of Cupertino, Mr. Steven Scharf. After receiving a positive response from the Cupertino City Council panel, I realized that “The Right Price” is an effective application for helping small businesses to survive and shine in a competitive economy.

Website for the Application: https://loquela-34ea1.web.app (Phone app built locally)

DevPost link: https://devpost.com/software/the-right-price-t4kipb

3. Hapi App

Web App and Phone App / July of 2018

I developed a web and mobile phone application called “Hapi App” that serves as a smart diary to help people cope with anxiety and depression. I was motivated to build this application when I observed that the competitive environment of my high school caused many students to become excessively stressed about their academic performance. “Hapi App” utilizes machine learning to provide users with an automated analysis to visualize their feelings about certain people, places, or things that are mentioned in a diary entry. “Hapi App” intelligently recommends a list of possible activities in order to help users strengthen relationships with people mentioned in the user’s diary entries. “Hapi App” also provides videos that are related to the user’s diary entries to help uplift the user’s spirits. In order to assess the effectiveness of “Hapi app,” I submitted my application to the Teen App Challenge Competition. Upon winning 1st place, I submitted my application along with a video recording of the application to the Congressional App Challenge National Competition and won 4th place. I was interviewed by Teen Services Librarian, Mr. Matt Lorenzo, and the video recording of my application is played daily on the television in the Cupertino library. “Hapi app” is also featured in an article on the Patch News website. After receiving positive feedback from these competitions, I realized that “Hapi app” provides an engaging medium to help people cope with psychological depression, a social injustice that affects all walks of life.

Website for the Application: http://www.hapidiary.com

Mentioned in News: https://patch.com/california/cupertino/cupertino-winners-teen-app-design-challenge-announced

4. Spanish Hero Educational Game

Educational Software / March of 2017

I developed a Spanish video game called the “Spanish Hero” that helps students to learn Spanish grammar in a fun way. I developed the computer game because I was flustered by the numerous Spanish grammar tenses and verb conjugations that I had to learn and remember in my Spanish 2 class. Therefore, “Spanish Hero” provided me with an entertaining medium to practice conjugating verbs into various Spanish grammar tenses. I have also made the “Spanish Hero” game and code available to the general public in order for them to learn Spanish grammar in a delightful manner. Technically, I utilized advanced computer programming concepts such as Object-oriented programming to organize the structure of the game's logic. This will allow other software developers to easily add more features to the game. Moreover, making my software public allows anyone to use it for improving their Spanish language skills. To gauge the effectiveness of my computer game, I presented the software to my Spanish teacher, Señora Griffin, who noted that the software was very ‘advanced'. It was also a smart way to teach Spanish concepts like verb conjugations to kids in poor communities, who do not have educational resources, like Spanish teachers and textbooks, to practice Spanish grammar exercises. The positive feedback of my Spanish teacher inspired me to create more applications to help others, such as “Hapi App” (Web App and phone application to help people who suffer from depression) and “The Right Price” (Web App and iPhone application to help local stores to gain popularity and assist customers in purchasing products of better quality).

Website to Download Application: https://spanishheromv.web.app/

Link to Source Code of the Software: https://github.com/rdeepak2002/SpanishHero/
